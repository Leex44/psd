PPA-PMO Western Leyte/Biliran

Transaction Process Flow

This portal provides a step-by-step guide to all transaction processes at the Philippine Ports Authority - Port Management Office of Western Leyte/Biliran.

Entrance Formalities

Vessel arrival and berthing procedures

1

Pre-Arrival Notification

Shipping agent submits Advance NOA (Notice of Arrival) and ABA (Application for Berth / Anchorage) prior Arrival of Vessel via:

iPorts System

Submission Timeline

Must be submitted at least 24 hours before vessel arrival for proper scheduling and preparation.

2

Proceed to Window 1 for Submission of Vessel Documents

Upon arrival, the vessel agent must complete the following:

  • Accomplish Dockage Report
  • Accomplish Vessel's Information Sheet (If the Vessel is not yet Registered to iPorts system)
  • Fill out Vessel's Logbook

Required Documents:

  • Vessel's Coasting Manifest
  • Vessel's Roll Book
  • Bill of Lading (for Wharfage Cargo on Board)
  • Application for Berth / Anchorage Form
  • Certificate of Registry (for Vessel arriving for the first time and not yet Registered to iPorts system)
3

Clearance from Other Agencies

Coordinate with the following agencies for necessary clearances:

Bureau of Customs (BOC)

For customs clearance and inspection of cargo

Office Location: Port Area

Bureau of Quarantine (BOQ)

For health and sanitation clearance

Contact: (053) 123-4567

Philippine Coast Guard (PCG)

For maritime safety inspection

Operating Hours: 8AM-5PM

MARINA (if applicable)

For vessel documentation verification

Email: marina@domain.com

Note: Some agencies may require additional documents specific to your vessel or cargo type. Please check with each agency for complete requirements.

4

Issuance of Entrance Clearance

PPA evaluates compliance with all requirements:

If Compliant

PPA issues Entrance Clearance within 1-2 hours after document verification

Vessel Berthing

Vessel is authorized to berth at the port according to assigned schedule

Applicable Fees

Fee Type Basis Rate
Dockage Fee Per GT per day ₱0.50
Berthing Fee Per GT ₱0.25

Cargo Withdrawal (Outbound/Import)

Procedures for withdrawing imported cargo from the port

1

Arrival of Import Cargo

Cargo is unloaded and stored in designated area with proper documentation

Cargo is documented and assessed by BOC for customs duties and taxes

Storage Timeline

Free storage period is 5 days from date of discharge. After this period, demurrage charges will apply.

2

Documentation

Importer or authorized representative submits the following documents:

Primary Documents

  • Original Bill of Lading
  • Import Entry and Internal Revenue Declaration (IEIRD)
  • Commercial Invoice

Supporting Documents

  • Official Receipt from BOC
  • PPA Wharfage Receipt
  • Delivery Order

Important: All documents must be properly notarized and authenticated. Photocopies must be certified true copies.

3

PPA Payment

Payment Methods

  • PPA Cashier

    Window 3, Ground Floor, PPA Office

  • Online System

    Via iPorts Payment Portal

  • Mobile Payment

    GCash, PayMaya available

Applicable Fees

  • Wharfage Charges 1% of CIF Value
  • Arrastre Charges ₱50-₱200 per ton
  • Storage Fees ₱10/day after free period

Discounts Available

Early payment (within 3 days of assessment) qualifies for 5% discount on wharfage charges.

4

Release Permit

PPA Issues

Gate Pass / Withdrawal Permit after payment verification

Authorization Requirements

If representative is claiming, must present:

  • Authorization Letter
  • Valid ID (Original + Copy)

Vehicle Requirements

For truck withdrawal, must present:

  • Vehicle Registration
  • Driver's License
5

Cargo Release

Final Step

Cargo is released and allowed to exit the port gate after inspection

Inspection Process

All cargoes are subject to random inspection by PPA and BOC personnel to verify:

  • Document-Verification
  • Quantity Verification
  • Condition Check

Gate Procedures

Upon exit, present the following to gate security:

  • Gate Pass
  • Driver's License
  • Vehicle Documents

Cargo Entry (Inbound/Export)

Procedures for bringing export cargo into the port

1

Pre-Loading Documents

Exporter or authorized representative submits the following documents:

Primary Documents

  • Export Declaration
  • Packing List
  • Commercial Invoice

Shipping Documents

  • Bill of Lading
  • Gate Pass Request
  • Export Permit (if applicable)

Note: All documents must be submitted in triplicate (original + 2 copies) for proper processing.

2

Inspection and Payment

Inspection Process

PPA and BOC inspect cargo for:

  • Document-Verification
  • Quantity Verification
  • Condition Check

Payment Process

Applicable fees include:

  • Handling/Arrastre Fees ₱50-₱200 per ton
  • Wharfage Fees 1% of FOB Value
  • Documentation Fees ₱100-₱500

Processing Timeline

Inspection and payment processing typically takes 2-4 hours during normal working days, depending on cargo volume.

3

Gate Entry Pass

PPA Issues

Cargo Entry Permit or Gate Pass after successful inspection and payment

Identification Requirements

Driver/trucker must present:

  • Valid Driver's License
  • Company ID (if applicable)

Vehicle Requirements

Vehicle must have:

  • Valid Registration
  • Roadworthiness
4

Cargo Loading

Final Step

Cargo enters the port and is loaded onto the vessel according to stowage plan

Loading Procedures

Loading operations follow:

  • Vessel Schedule
  • Safety Protocols
  • Supervision by PPA

Cargo Handling

Special handling for:

  • Perishable Goods
  • Hazardous Materials
  • Oversized Cargo

Other Permits

Additional permits and clearances for port activities

A

Permits for Port Activities

Includes the following activities requiring special permits:

Maintenance Work

For repairs and facility maintenance within port premises

Processing Time: 1-2 working days

Surveys

For inspections and assessments by third parties

Processing Time: 3-5 working days

Photography/Filming

For media and documentation purposes

Processing Time: 3 working days

Utility Installation

For electrical and other utility works

Processing Time: 5-7 working days
B

Application Process

Step 1: Submit Application

Submit Letter of Intent or Permit Application at PPA Administrative Office, Window 5

Step 2: Attach Requirements

  • Valid ID (Original + Copy)
  • Authorization Letter (if representative)
  • Safety Plan (for hazardous activities)

Step 3: Pay Applicable Fees

Fees vary depending on activity type and duration. Minimum fee is ₱500.

Step 4: PPA Review

PPA reviews application within the specified processing time.

Step 5: Issuance of Permit

Approved permits can be claimed at Window 5 upon presentation of payment receipt.

Important: All permits are non-transferable and valid only for the specified activity, date, and location. Any changes must be reported to PPA for approval.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries about port transactions

The PPA-PMO office is open from Monday to Friday, 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM, with no noon break. For vessel-related transactions outside these hours, please coordinate with the Port Operations Division.

  • iPorts tracking system (online)
  • PPA Customer Service Hotline: (053) 123-4567
  • Email inquiry to pmo-westernleyte@ppa.com.ph
  • In-person inquiry at Window 7 (Information Desk)
